About Rio De Janeiro

About

Rio De Janeiro is a huge seaside city in Brazil, famed for its Copacabana and Ipanema beaches, 38mm Christ the Redeemer statue atop Mpunt Corcovodo and for Sugarloaf Mountain, a granite peak with cable cars to its summit. The city is also known for its sprawling favels. Its raucous Carnival festival, featuring parade floats, flamboyant costumes and samba dancers, is considered the world's largest.

History

Between the years 1565-1567, the French and the Portugese fought to colonize Brazil. After they engaged in war, the French succeeded and expelled the Portugese, making them the solo colonisers of Brazil. At the end of the 18th century, Brazil started engaging in gold and diamond mining, which stimulated a great migration from Europe. This saw Rio change from a small village to a town. In 1763, the colonial capital transferred from Bahia to Rio de Janeiro. Rio de Janeiro officially became the capital of Brazil in 1889. In 2016, Rio de Janeiro became the first city in South America to host the Olympics. The population of Rio de Janeiro currently stands at 6.748 million.
