Lifestyle

Climate

Rio is a steamy, tropical city with high humidity levels year-round. The climate in Rio is hot all year long and temperatures barely go below 20 degrees celcius, which is good for people who love summer.

Language

Most people in Rio de Janeiro speak Portugese but most of them have an understanding of the English language so communication should not be a problem. It is recommended that if you are to visit, you learn a bit of Portugese or have one of the locals accompany you.

Religion

Brazillians are mostly Catholics or Anglican. Brazil is currently the world's largest Catholic country. They are well known for the statue of Christ the Redeemer and for their astounding churches.

Food

Rio de Janeiro has excellent restuarants that serve all types of cuisine. Feijao (Black Beans) and rice with roast meat, vegetables and farofa (toasted manioc flour) is one of the more popular dishes. Churrascarias are also very popular, you help yourself to a salad and a series of different barbequed meats is brought to you till you cannot eat anymore. Seafood is also very good. Brazillian puddings are invaribly very sweet and often include doce de leite which is a kind of caramel derived from condensed milk.
